Origins of R&B

R&B originated in the 1940's as a fusion of Jazz, blues and gospel. It was mostly consumed by African Americans and produced by African Americans. During the 1950's through the 1970's most of the bands instruments would consist of a piano, one or two guitars, bass, drums, one or more saxophones and sometimes vocalists. In the 1980's R&B had a new style called "Contemporary R&B" which consisted of of jazz, soul, funk, disco, blues and electronic.
